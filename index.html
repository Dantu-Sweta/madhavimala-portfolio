A Life in Dance

Five decades of classical artistry

◆

For nearly five decades, Madhavi Mala has brought the stories of gods, goddesses, and legends to life through Kuchipudi and Bharatanatyam. With 49 years as a performing artist and 34 years as a choreographer, she is an empanelled artist with the Indian Council for Cultural Relations (ICCR) and a Graded artist of Doordarshan, India's national television network.

She has performed extensively across Europe and Asia, served as a panel judge for grading classical dance artists at Doordarshan Hyderabad, and as a film censor jury member. A popular anchor on Eenaadu and Doordarshan, she has delivered lecture demonstrations on Kuchipudi in New Delhi and Raipur organized by IRCEN, and conducted workshops across the USA and India teaching her signature choreographies.

Equally at home in male and female lead roles, she is celebrated for portraying Lord Krishna and Lord Shiva as compellingly as Goddess Durga and Alamelumanga.
